About Me

I’m Dr. Fatma Bicenturk, a dedicated researcher and content creator with a PhD in Performing Arts from Canterbury Christ Church University. My journey began with a deep passion for understanding the impact of creative movement on young minds, leading me to explore the intersection of dance, childhood development, and holistic well-being.

With a rich background in both academic research and practical experience, I’ve expanded my focus to include a wide range of topics that inspire growth and balance. Through "The Holistic Space," I share insights on creative expression, health, wellness, and personal development. My goal is to empower individuals of all ages to discover the transformative power of movement, mindfulness, and holistic living.

Whether you're interested in dance, childhood development, mental health, or overall well-being, "The Holistic Space" offers resources and guidance to support your journey towards a vibrant and balanced life.

Education

PhD - Performing Arts (2019-2024)

Canterbury Christ Church University

My doctoral research focuses on the impact of creative dance on early childhood development. I am exploring how movement and artistic expression can enhance cognitive, creative, and social growth in children. This research aims to integrate performing arts into educational frameworks to support holistic development.

MA - Arts and Cultural Management (2017-2018)

Canterbury Christ Church University

During my master’s studies, I gained extensive knowledge in managing arts and cultural projects, with a focus on dance and curating. My thesis, “Street Dance: A Reflection of Gender Equality & Stereotypes,” explored how street dance challenges gender norms, focusing on Cyprus. I also enhanced my curatorial skills through museum visits and exhibitions, deepening my understanding of dance as a tool for social commentary and community engagement.

BA - Performing Arts (Dance) (2013-2017)

Girne American University - Pera

My undergraduate studies provided a strong foundation in various forms of dance and performance. I focused on the practical and theoretical aspects of dance, developing my skills in choreography and performance. My final project involved creating and performing a dance piece that explored themes of identity and self-expression.
